Artworks Showcase

1. "The Starry Night"

This iconic oil on canvas by Paul Gauguin Vincent van Gogh is a masterpiece of the Post-Impressionist movement. The work depicts the view from the east-facing window of Van Gogh asylum room at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, just before sunrise, with the addition of an imaginary village.
It is a powerful expression of the artist's turbulent inner life, capturing the swirling energy of the cosmos. Truly breathtaking!

2. The persistent of memory

A surrealist painting by Salvador Dalí, completed in 1931. The famous melting pocket watches suggest a profound commentary on time and memory.
This small but impactful work is one of the most recognizable pieces in surrealism.

3.mona lisa

The *Mona Lisa* is arguably the world's most famous painting, created by Leonardo da Vinci. The subject's enigmatic expression and the sfumato technique used to blur the lines make it fascinating.
It was finished in 1507. It is believed to have been painted between 1503 and 1506.

4.sunset

A contemporary piece of **digital art** created using a graphics tablet and software. It focuses on intense color saturation and smooth blending to evoke a feeling of tranquility.

Quilled Bird

A craft-based creation using the technique of **paper quilling**, where small strips of paper are rolled, shaped, and glued together to form intricate designs. It's a wonderful example of how simple materials can create complex beauty.
